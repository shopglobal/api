Join the Contest

Get Started

The competition begins on August 9, 2017 and ends at 23:59 Eastern US time on September 20, 2017. Anyone from anywhere in the world who has a Freelancer.com account can join. Those who have no account yet can register for free. Submit your video via the Freelancer contest page and vie for the $10,000 Grand Prize or for one of the 9 other awesome rewards. Check out the previous Freelancer.com Expose Our Logo contest for inspiration.

Terms and Conditions

  • All contest entries must present Freelancer.com in a positive light. Entries featuring illegal acts or acts that may bring Freelancer.com into disrepute will be rejected. Entries with offensive graphics and/or words will also be rejected.
  • All contest entries must feature the "Freelancer.com" version of the logo available in the media kit.
  • Group entries are permitted (if you wish to form a team by creating a group account).
  • We will require every contest entry to be publicly viewable on the Expose Our Logo 2017 Contest Page as interesting entries may be shared with external media.
  • All entries submitted for the contest will remain the property of Freelancer.com and will not be returned to the participants.
  • Video Requirements:
    • Accepted formats: *.mov; *.avi; *.mpeg
    • Maximum length: 5 mins
    • Size: 1920x1080, 30, 60 fps
    • Encoding: H/264, Quicktime, Appleprores
    • Audio encoding: MP3
    • Audio: 64 kb/s minimum

    As much as possible, please include a mixture of shots and angles: a close-up, an extreme close-up, a shot of you at work, or even flat lay shots.

    Tips and pointers: Shoot with a stabilizer, rig, or tripod. Include various amounts of shots and angles. Include some slow-motion shots to add more to your submission. Your footage should be in high resolution.

  • All video submissions should be shot in 16:9 aspect ratio. They should be center cut protected. Furthermore, avoid overlaying text on the video or parts of the video where the Freelancer logo is showcased.
  • For broadcast purposes, the finalists may be asked to provide the raw unedited files used during production of the video. This may include uncompressed QuickTime files, final audio mix and/or separate audio tracks for dialogue, music, and effects. Please hold onto all of your files until the end of the competition.
  • We reserve the right to modify these rules at any time.

Participation

We are inviting everyone to join, wherever you are in the world. Contestants must be registered participants of Freelancer.com. Those who don't have a Freelancer account yet can sign up to participate in this exciting competition.

Submission Format

  • Accepted formats: *.mov; *.avi; *.mpeg
  • Maximum length: 5 mins
  • Size: 1920x1080, 30, 60 fps
  • Encoding: H/264, Quicktime, Appleprores
  • Audio encoding: MP3
  • Audio: 64 kb/s minimum

Dates

You have until 23:59 Eastern US time on September 20, 2017 to submit your entry. Within 14 days after the closing date, we will review all the submissions and select the finalists. The announcement of winners will be on October 4.

Conditions of Entry

Any entry that is clearly and objectively insulting, sexist, offensive, racist, crude, intolerant, and in any other way deemed "inappropriate" will be automatically disqualified. You can enter as many times as you like. By entering the contest, you agree that all entries submitted for the contest will remain the property of Freelancer.com and will not be returned to the participants.

All entries must place Freelancer.com in a positive light. Entries with offensive graphics and/or words or acts that will bring Freelancer.com into disrepute will not be accepted. Entries which do not meet the required Submission Format will be automatically disqualified from the contest. Freelancer.com is not liable in any manner for entries lost, delayed, or damaged.

Prize funds will be paid out to the winners through Freelancer.com. Thus, participants must have a valid Freelancer.com account to participate in the contest. Entry deadlines may be extended at the discretion of the organisers. The organisers reserve the right not to award a specific prize category at its own discretion if the entries are deemed inappropriate or of poor quality.
